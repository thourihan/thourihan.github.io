About Tim Hourihan

I'm a Research Programmer at Boston Fusion, where I integrate and adapt machine learning models for computer vision applications in national defense. I work with architectures like transformers and joint embedding predictive architectures (JEPAs), modifying them to meet our specific needs.

Before joining Boston Fusion, I graduated from the University of Rhode Island in 2024 with a Bachelor of Science in Computer Science and a minor in Data Science, earning a 3.99 GPA. While in school, I worked as a Software Developer at OMNI Digital Services, building full-stack websites and internal tools with JavaScript, PHP, and MySQL. I also completed a Software Development Co-op at FM Global, contributing to a large-scale internal web application using React and C#.

Outside of work, I’ve contributed to a range of academic and personal projects involving machine learning, software development, and game design. Some of my favorites include a deepfake detection system using EfficientNet and FasterViT that placed first in a machine learning course competition and a sentiment analysis visualization built during HackMIT, where my team earned third place in the MIT-IBM Watson AI Track. I’ve also published several games on itch.io using Unity and Godot.

I truly enjoy solving challenging technical problems and I'm always interested in learning more and growing as a developer in this field.
